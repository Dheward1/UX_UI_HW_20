Project Overview

Working as a UX designer on this project with two of my classmates, Em and Jean, We were tasked with redesigning a non-profit website. We wanted a challenge so we chose Ballet West based on Em’s negative experience with the website in the past.

Why are we redesigning Ballet West?

balletwest.org has a confusing navigation system, underwhelming visuals, a convoluted ticket buying and money donation process.

Organization Description

Ballet West is an American ballet company based in Salt Lake City, Utah. It was founded in 1963 as the Utah Civic Ballet by Willam F. Christensen, the company's first artistic director, and Glenn Walker Wallace, who served as its first president.

We first did a Heuristic Evaluation of the current website to become familiar with the product we were going to be redesigning. We found a lot that didn't follow any heuristic rules such as

Empathize

We tested six particiapants on different tasks for the current website to gather any user insights. Our User Observations gave us qualitative data we could use later in the ideation phase. We wanted to see how users interacted with the current website to pick up on behaviors and hear them think out loud while we had them do some tasks to mine for pain points.

“The link to ‘Click here for single tickets’ is the same as the link below. It's confusing and unnecessary”

“Not every link looks the same and the gold makes me wonder if it is a link or a headline.”

“They clearly want you to sign up for a subscription. But what is the subscription?”

“The fonts look really funny. I don't know which are links.”

"Single tickets is mentioned too many times without quickly knowing where to click.”

“Was expecting a lot of videos of performances”

To better get to know our user we took the data we found in the User Observations and brainstormed on our empathy map to better understand our user and to help come up with our problem statement later on

After we had a solid understanding of the problems facing the current website we wanted to interview users to gather insights for WHY a user would attend the Ballet, Donate and how we might be able to increase donor participation. We then synthesized this data into an affinity diagram.

Hypothesis

"We believe we can help donor engagement with BalletWest.org and the visibility within the ballet community as a whole by adding improved visuals to the website, Video highlights and Spotlighting dancers."

After analyzing all the data we gathered from our research we were introduced to Julie Hansen. Problem Statement

"The current Ballet West website does not showcase their dancers, productions, nor their creative processes in a visually appealing way. This leads to lower engagement with the website and causes frustustrations to possible donors and ticket-buyers."

User Flows

We determined that the most common activity on the Ballet West website would be to purchase tickets and to donate, so here, we established two flows that we would reference to create our prototypes.

Sketches

From our heuristic evaluation, we knew that the mobile version of the original website was not great... so we really wanted to go with the 'mobile first' mindset. We tried to design everything in cards so it will translate easily between mobile and desktop versions of the website.

Style Guide

We wanted to honor the original brand colors of Ballet West since they did recently do a rebranding, so we kept the black, gold, and white. But to help increase readability, we changed the background color to black, and tried to use the gold really as an accent color instead of it being the primary header color. We chose one display font as the large header, and the rest will be a variation of the sans serif font, Lato.

Wireframes and Testing

We wireframed two different Nav menus to test out to see if our users preferred the larger or smaller icons.

Final Thoughts

What we learned

  • Interviewing more qualified candidates during our research would have provided better insights.
  • Coming up with a whole design system is a lot of work! Wish we had more time to spend on this before building out our wireframes.
  • Testing is crucial and we definitely could have gone a dozen more sessions testing and iterating before getting to a place that was ready to go live

Moving Forward

  • Revisiting User interviews with qualified candidates to discover better insights
  • Iterate on the app and test each function before moving to Hi-Fi
  • Develop and test the Donation portal
  • Document more to illustrate our process
